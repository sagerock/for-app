The F‑square and the 4‑square

A first crack at Jamie's idea — one square shape, used twice. The F is a square that opens to the right; the 4 is the same square opening on top. Same shape, rotated a quarter turn. Three directions below; nothing precious, all clay.
The move: take a single squarish bracket and turn it. Opening to the right reads as an F; opening upward reads as a 4. Placed together they make the mark — and in one version they interlock, the 4 filling exactly where the F opens.
